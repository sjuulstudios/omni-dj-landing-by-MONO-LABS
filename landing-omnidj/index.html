FAQ

Honest answers, short.

What is "the drop" exactly?

The peak moment after a buildup, where the energy spikes. In dance music it's usually the highest point of the track. Omni DJ detects those automatically, including buildups and breakdowns around them, so you get the full impact in the clip.

Will it work on my Mac?

Omni DJ runs on macOS 12 Monterey or later. Both Intel and Apple Silicon. We test on Apple Silicon (M1/M2/M3) primarily, performance is best there. For a 1h set, analysis takes around 3 minutes on M1 Pro.

What about my unreleased tracks?

Local first by design. Your audio is processed entirely on your Mac. Nothing uploads. The only data Omni DJ sends to our servers is your account info and metadata about clips you export. Your audio stays yours.

Is there a Windows version?

Not yet. We're focused on macOS first to ship a single great version. Windows is on the roadmap but won't ship during the beta.

Can I post to TikTok and Instagram directly from Omni DJ?

Soon. The beta exports clips to your Downloads folder so you can post manually. Scheduled posting through Postiz is in development and ships in a Pro update during the beta period.

How do I report a bug?

Two ways. Use the in-app feedback button (sidebar footer), or post in the #bugs channel of our Discord. We respond within 48 hours during the beta.

What happens after the beta?

Pricing kicks in. Beta users keep access to a Free tier with reasonable limits. Pro and Studio plans unlock the advanced features (auto-mode, multi-artist, scheduled posting). Beta participants get the first month of Pro on us.

Who's behind Omni DJ?

MONO LABS, a small studio in the Netherlands. Built by a DJ who got tired of editing clips at 3am after a set.
